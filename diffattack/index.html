DiffAttack: Imperceptible and Transferable Audio Adversarial Attack via Diffusion model

Abstract

Recently, adversarial attacks on audio have garnered significant interest. However, existing methods often produce audio adversarial samples that are perceptible to human ears. To bridge this gap, we introduce a novel approach named DiffAttack, which employs a diffusion model for adversarial attacks. Initially, we extract the Mel spectrogram of the original audio. Subsequently, the original audio is encoded into latent code via forward diffusion process. Finally, this latent code is reconstructed into an adversarial audio through the reverse diffusion process, conditioned on the modified Mel spectrogram. Experimental evaluations conducted on ECAPA and ResNet, both advanced speaker recognition models, reveal that our method surpasses state-of-the-art techniques in attack success rate, transferability and auditory quality.
